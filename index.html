source
notice

Chronic-condition cohorts are derived from patient self-report collected via the PreventScripts health-risk assessment instrument. These cohort assignments have not been adjudicated against payer claims, provider EHR problem lists, ICD-10 diagnostic coding, or third-party clinical registries. Condition prevalence, cohort sizes, and all condition-segmented metrics should be interpreted as program-reported rather than clinically confirmed. Independent validation is required for payer negotiation, risk-adjustment modeling, or clinical attribution.

Sex / biological gender is not currently captured in the PreventScripts production schema (no sex, gender, or equivalent column on users, assessment_attempts, or related tables). Sex-stratified analysis cannot be produced from the current data source. Ethnicity is captured on assessment_attempts and is available as an alternative demographic dimension if required. Recommendation: add a sex field to the intake assessment for future cohort stratification.

Methods, definitions & data-quality notes

Chronic-condition attribution — official disclosure

All chronic-condition cohorts are sourced from patient self-report via the PreventScripts health-risk assessment instrument. These are not provider-adjudicated, EHR-verified, ICD-10-coded, or payer-claims-confirmed diagnoses. Independent validation required for payer-facing use.

Sex / gender data gap

Sex and gender are not captured in the PreventScripts production schema. No sex, gender, or equivalent column exists on users, assessment_attempts, or related tables. Sex-stratified analysis cannot be produced from current data. Ethnicity is available on assessment_attempts as an alternative demographic dimension. Recommendation: add sex to the intake assessment for future stratification.

CPT 99445 / 99454 framework

99454 reimburses 16+ unique days of device data transmission per calendar month. 99445 (new CY 2026, per CMS 2026 Final Rule per the PreventScripts 2026 billing & coding manual) reimburses 2–15 unique days. Patients bill one or the other in a given month. "Reading day" = distinct calendar date with ≥ 1 BP, glucose, or weight reading.

Weight-loss & blood-sugar outcome methodology

Weight loss: first recorded weight in rolling 180d vs. most recent recorded weight, patients with ≥ 2 readings. Clinically-significant loss threshold: ≥ 5% per USPSTF. Two unit-entry outliers flagged in <50 cohort inflating the average-change statistic; median is more representative. Blood sugar: 90-day average fingerstick glucose per patient, compared to clinical thresholds (≤ 140 controlled, > 180 elevated, > 300 crisis). No HbA1c in schema — fingerstick only.

Lifestyle plan & survey mechanics

Active plan = plans.status='Active' AND plans.archived=0. A patient may have multiple historical plans; counts use most recent active plan per patient unless otherwise stated. Weekly survey response rate = surveys with status='Attempted' ÷ total surveys sent. Progress sentiment is self-reported on a 5-point scale (Terrible / Not Great / OK / Pretty Well / Wonderful).

Data-quality flags

  • Assessment "% completed" pre-Dec '24 shows 100% due to platform not persisting "sent but not completed" rows. Post-Dec '24 is reliable baseline.
  • user_events.fk_user_id NULL across all rows — mobile app adherence unmeasurable.
  • user_visits has no session-duration field — 99457 20-min threshold not directly measurable.
  • No HbA1c table — T2DM outcomes limited to fingerstick glucose.
  • 114 historical un-enrollments lack captured reason (pre-2025).
  • Depression not captured in condition taxonomy.
  • Weight data contains suspected unit-entry outliers (lb vs kg or typo) — add input validator.
  • Weekly-survey response rate is 2.8% all-time — channel effectively non-functional for data collection.
  • 103 of 439 active RPM patients (23.5%) lack a matched assessment.
  • Cells with n < 20 flagged with ⚠.
  • Filters (Age / Condition) highlight matching cells; they do not recompute totals. Year filter re-scopes KPI tile values and chart highlights.
